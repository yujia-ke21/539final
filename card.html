Feeling lost in Ann Arbor?

Just flip a card to unveil exciting things to do in the city!

Card layout

1.Yost Ice Arena

Go ice skating at the Yost Ice Arena, home to the University of Michigan ice hockey team.

2.Tiny Lion Cat Cafe

Experience a cozy lounge filled with friendly cats at the Tiny Lions lounge and adoption Center (TLC) in Ann Arbor, where you can pet, play with, and even adopt these lovable felines while enjoying the café's amenities such as free Wi-Fi, cat-lovers store, and rental space for special occasions.

3.Farmers Market

Explore the Ann Arbor Farmers Market, a bustling marketplace with fresh produce, artisanal goods, and more.

4.The Rocket

The Rocket is a fun candy store with a vast selection of bulk and wrapped candies, chocolates, and nostalgic treats like jumbo peanut butter cups, chocolate-covered cherries, and more.

5.Encore Records Store

Encore records store is a haven for music enthusiasts, offering a wide selection of records, tapes, CDs, DVDs, sheet music, books, and memorabilia.

6..Huron River

Take a scenic walk through the Huron River and Gallup Park, a beautiful park with a river, canoeing, and fishing.

7.UM Museum of Natural History

Visit the University of Michigan Museum of Natural History, where you can learn about dinosaurs, Michigan wildlife, and more.

8.Michigan Stadium

Of course, we can't talk about Ann Arbor and the University of Michigan without mentioning football.“The Big House” stadium, the largest in the country, hosts over 100,000 passionate fans during rivalry games against Michigan State or Ohio State.

9.Border-to-Border Trail

Take a bike ride on the Border-to-Border Trail, a scenic trail that runs from Ypsilanti to Dexter.

10.BIG Game Center

Big Game Center is a wonderland of entertainment with a thrilling assortment of attractions such as arcade games, billiards, bumper cars, and an indoor playground that are sure to keep visitors of all ages captivated and endlessly amused.

11.Michigan Theater

Catch a concert or show at the Michigan Theater, a historic venue that hosts a variety of performances throughout the year.

12.Kerrytown

Kerrytown is a historic district in Ann Arbor known for its locally owned businesses that offer online shopping, curbside pick-up, and in-store options to make shopping safe and easy, making it a retail destination in the area.

13.Hands-On Museum

Visit the Ann Arbor Hands-On Museum, a family-friendly museum with interactive exhibits on science, technology, and more.

14.Pinckney Recreation Area

Go on a hike in the Pinckney Recreation Area, which includes trails, lakes, and forests.

15.Nichols Arboretum

Take a stroll through the Nichols Arboretum, a beautiful park with trails, gardens, and a stunning peony garden.

16.AA Film Festival

Attend the Ann Arbor Film Festival, a world-renowned festival featuring independent and experimental films.

17.AA Art Center

Take a pottery class at the Ann Arbor Art Center or one of the many other studios in the area.

18.Northern Michigan

If you're looking for a beautiful autumn destination, Northern Michigan boasts stunning maple trees showcasing vibrant colors, and visitors can also indulge in delicious maple syrup made by local producers during their fall visit.

19.Zingerman's Delicatesse

Zingerman's Deli is famous for its mouth-watering Reuben sandwich, a delectable combination of corned beef, sauerkraut, Swiss cheese, and rye bread, that was praised by former President Obama during his visit to the University of Michigan in 2014.

20.UM Museum of Art

The University of Michigan Museum of Art is a renowned art museum located in Ann Arbor, Michigan, featuring a vast collection of over 21,000 artworks spanning a wide range of time periods and cultures.

21.Blank Slate Creamery

Blank Slate Creamery is a renowned ice cream shop that serves delectably creamy, homemade, and all-natural ice creams, sorbets, and non-dairy options, among which the most loved ones are Madagascar Vanilla, Non-Dairy Peppermint Bark, and Hot Cocoa.

22.University of Michigan Ann Arbor

The University of Michigan Ann Arbor is an exceptional institution with a rich history, diverse community, and beautiful campus, making it a great destination to visit.

23.Matthaei Botanical Gardens

Matthaei Botanical Gardens is a breathtaking natural oasis located in Ann Arbor that features a wide range of stunning plant collections, walking trails, and educational programs.

24.Main Street & Nickels Arcade

Main Street and Nickels Arcade in Ann Arbor are bustling shopping districts where visitors can browse a variety of unique shops, boutiques, and art galleries.

25.Bon bon bon

Bon Bon Bon is a delectable chocolate shop located in Nickels Arcade, Ann Arbor, where visitors can indulge in a variety of artisanal chocolate treats.

26.Cannelle by Matt Knio

Cannelle by Matt Knio, a New French Patisserie from Detroit, has expanded to Ann Arbor and is known for their delicious almond croissants, chocolate cakes, and signature pastry, the canelle.

27.Ann Arbor Summer Festival

The Ann Arbor Summer Festival, held every June - July, is a vibrant celebration of art, music, food, and culture, drawing in thousands of people from all over Michigan and beyond.

28.Kelsey Museum of Archaeology

The Kelsey Museum of Archaeology is a must-visit for anyone interested in ancient civilizations, with its vast collection of over 100,000 artifacts from around the world.

29.Stonebridge Golf Club

The Stonebridge Golf Club offers a beautiful and challenging course surrounded by nature, with options for golf lessons, a driving range, and a bar and grill to enjoy after your game.

30.Chateau Aeronautique Winery

With its unique combination of wine and airplanes, Chateau Aeronautique Winery in Jackson, Michigan, is a must-visit destination near Ann Arbor for those looking for an unusual and unforgettable experience.
