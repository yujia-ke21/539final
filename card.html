Feeling lost in Ann Arbor?

Just flip a card to unveil exciting things to do in the city!

1.Yost Ice Arena

Go ice skating at the Yost Ice Arena, home to the University of Michigan ice hockey team.

2.Tiny Lion Cat Cafe

Experience a cozy lounge filled with friendly cats at the Tiny Lions lounge and adoption Center (TLC) in Ann Arbor, where you can pet, play with, and even adopt these lovable felines while enjoying the café's amenities such as free Wi-Fi, cat-lovers store, and rental space for special occasions.

3.Farmers Market

Explore the Ann Arbor Farmers Market, a bustling marketplace with fresh produce, artisanal goods, and more.

4.The Rocket

The Rocket is a fun candy store with a vast selection of bulk and wrapped candies, chocolates, and nostalgic treats like jumbo peanut butter cups, chocolate-covered cherries, and more.

5.Encore Records Store

Encore records store is a haven for music enthusiasts, offering a wide selection of records, tapes, CDs, DVDs, sheet music, books, and memorabilia.

6..Huron River

Take a scenic walk through the Huron River and Gallup Park, a beautiful park with a river, canoeing, and fishing.

7.UM Museum of Natural History

Visit the University of Michigan Museum of Natural History, where you can learn about dinosaurs, Michigan wildlife, and more.

8.Michigan Stadium

Of course, we can't talk about Ann Arbor and the University of Michigan without mentioning football.“The Big House” stadium, the largest in the country, hosts over 100,000 passionate fans during rivalry games against Michigan State or Ohio State.

9.Border-to-Border Trail

Take a bike ride on the Border-to-Border Trail, a scenic trail that runs from Ypsilanti to Dexter.

10.BIG Game Center

Big Game Center is a wonderland of entertainment with a thrilling assortment of attractions such as arcade games, billiards, bumper cars, and an indoor playground that are sure to keep visitors of all ages captivated and endlessly amused.

11.Michigan Theater

Catch a concert or show at the Michigan Theater, a historic venue that hosts a variety of performances throughout the year.

12.Kerrytown

Kerrytown is a historic district in Ann Arbor known for its locally owned businesses that offer online shopping, curbside pick-up, and in-store options to make shopping safe and easy, making it a retail destination in the area.

13.Hands-On Museum

Visit the Ann Arbor Hands-On Museum, a family-friendly museum with interactive exhibits on science, technology, and more.

15.Nichols Arboretum
